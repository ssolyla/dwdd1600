Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the responsible, safe, and ethical way to use technology, encompassing behaviors like protecting privacy, understanding digital law, practicing online etiquette, verifying information, and engaging respectfully in digital communities to promote well-being and positive change, similar to being a good citizen offline.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law, also known as cyber law, is the body of legal principles and regulations governing activities, rights, and responsibilities in digital environments, covering issues from data privacy and cybersecurity to intellectual property and e-commerce, essentially setting rules for ethical and legal conduct online.

What is AUP?

AUP most commonly stands for Acceptable Use Policy, a set of rules defining permitted and prohibited actions for using an organization's digital resources like networks, computers, and the internet, ensuring security, compliance, and proper conduct

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the act of presenting someone else's work, words, ideas, or data as your own without proper acknowledgment or citation

What is Academic Dishonesty?

cademic dishonesty is any deceptive behavior in an academic setting, like cheating, plagiarism, or fabrication, where someone gains an unfair advantage by taking credit for work that isn't theirs or submitting false information, violating institutional rules and ethical standards.

What are The Rules for AI Generated Content?

Rules for AI-generated content focus on transparency, accountability, and ethical usage, requiring disclosure of AI tools, human review for accuracy, and respecting copyright.
